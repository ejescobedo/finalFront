Park Surveillance

The City of El Paso Parks and Recreation Department is increasingly having several incidents of vandalism occuring in their parks after hours. From this, resulting damage has escalated to individual acts costing the city and taxpayers excess of $250,000 per incident. This does not include the cost of resources spent on rerouting police officers and purchasing additional deterrent methods. The department responsible for managing and litigating these cases has stated that they do not have sufficient manpower or the budget to keep security at these parks overnight after the park has closed.
